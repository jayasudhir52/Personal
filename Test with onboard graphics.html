Test with Onboard Graphics

Why Test with Onboard Graphics?

  • Helps determine if a dedicated GPU is causing display issues.
  • Useful when troubleshooting black screens, artifacts, or crashes.
  • Ensures the system can display output without relying on the external GPU.
  • Assists in isolating hardware problems and driver conflicts.

Steps to Test with Onboard Graphics

  • Power off the PC and unplug it from the outlet.
  • Remove the dedicated GPU from the PCIe slot if installed.
  • Connect your monitor to the motherboardâ€™s video output (HDMI, DisplayPort, DVI, or VGA).
  • Power on the PC and enter BIOS/UEFI to ensure onboard graphics are enabled.
  • Boot into the operating system and check if display output works correctly.
  • If successful, this confirms the dedicated GPU may need troubleshooting or replacement.

Additional Tips

Update motherboard graphics drivers for the best performance. If the onboard display works fine, try reseating the GPU, checking power connectors, or testing the GPU in another system.
