Check Power Cable and PSU

Check Power Cable

  • Ensure the power cable is firmly connected to both the PC and the wall socket.
  • Try a different wall outlet to rule out socket issues.
  • Inspect the cable for cuts, bends, or exposed wires.
  • Replace the cable if any physical damage is found.
  • Test using another known working power cable.
  • Plug the PC directly into the wall socket instead of a power strip.

Check PSU (Power Supply Unit)

  • Ensure the PSU power switch at the back is set to ON (I).
  • Verify the 24-pin motherboard power connector is properly seated.
  • Check the CPU power connector (4-pin or 8-pin) is securely connected.
  • Listen for PSU fan movement or electrical sounds when powering on.
  • Check for any burnt smell or visible damage from the PSU.
  • Test the system using a PSU tester or a known working PSU.
