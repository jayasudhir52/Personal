Check Monitor & Cable

Why Check Monitor & Cable?

  • Ensures the display receives a proper signal from the PC.
  • Prevents flickering, black screen, or distorted visuals.
  • Helps identify faulty cables or monitor issues early.
  • Improves overall user experience and display reliability.

Steps to Check Monitor & Cable

  • Turn off the PC and monitor before inspecting connections.
  • Ensure the video cable (HDMI, DisplayPort, DVI, VGA) is securely connected to both the PC and monitor.
  • Inspect the cable for visible damage, bent pins, or frayed connectors.
  • If possible, test the monitor with a different cable or another PC to rule out issues.
  • Ensure the monitor is set to the correct input/source.
  • Turn on the PC and monitor, and check if the display works correctly.

Additional Tips

Using high-quality cables and avoiding excessive bending or stretching helps prevent signal issues. Regularly check connections if you move your setup or upgrade hardware.
